About PrintLab

Hardware meets software.

PrintLab is a documentation system built on a JSON data layer — a lightweight CMS concept that requires no backend, no database, and no server. Every project is stored as structured data and rendered dynamically.

This project came from real work. At Ampoly Plastics in 2025, I designed and tested functional machine parts using CAD software and a 3D printer — solving live industrial challenges with hands-on prototyping.

PrintLab exists to document that process — proving that physical and digital skills are not separate disciplines, but deeply connected ones.

The Process

How every print gets made.

Good 3D printing is 80% design and 20% printing. Every project in PrintLab follows the same disciplined workflow — from sketch to final part.

01

Define the Brief

What does the part need to do? What are the load, fit, and material requirements? This stage prevents 90% of print failures.

02

CAD Modelling

Design in CAD software with precise tolerances — accounting for print shrinkage, layer adhesion, and assembly clearances.

03

Slice & Configure

Slicer settings — layer height, infill, supports, and orientation — are as important as the design itself. Document everything.

04

Test & Iterate

First prints rarely fit perfectly. Measure, adjust tolerances, reprint. Iteration is the process, not a sign of failure.
